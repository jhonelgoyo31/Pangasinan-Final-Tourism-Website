Pista'y Dayat

  • Location: Lingayen Gulf and coastal towns of Pangasinan
  • Month celebrated: May
  • Category: Coastal Festival / Cultural Celebration
  • Best for: Beach lovers, cultural tourists, environmental awareness trips

Pista'y Dayat, meaning “Sea Festival,” is Pangasinan’s annual thanksgiving celebration for the sea. It honors the province’s coastal resources, fishermen, and communities whose lives depend on the ocean.

The festival is one of the most important cultural events in Pangasinan, celebrating the connection between people and the sea through tourism activities, cultural shows, and environmental programs.

What is Pista’y Dayat?

Pista’y Dayat is a traditional coastal festival held every May. It is a thanksgiving event dedicated to the sea, which is a vital source of livelihood, food, and identity for the people of Pangasinan.

The celebration highlights the importance of protecting marine resources while promoting tourism and local culture.

Festival Activities

During Pista’y Dayat, various activities are held across coastal towns such as Lingayen, Dagupan, and nearby beach areas.

  • Beach clean-up drives and environmental campaigns
  • Cultural street dancing and parades
  • Food fairs featuring seafood and local delicacies
  • Boat races and coastal sports activities
  • Concerts and evening entertainment programs

Cultural and Environmental Importance

The festival is not only a celebration but also a reminder of the importance of marine conservation. It promotes awareness about protecting seas, mangroves, and coastal ecosystems.

Fisherfolk communities play a major role in the celebration, showcasing their traditions, livelihood, and relationship with the sea.

Where It is Celebrated

The main celebrations usually take place in Lingayen Gulf and extend to coastal towns such as Lingayen, Dagupan City, Binmaley, and other nearby areas.

Each town adds its own unique cultural programs, making the festival diverse and vibrant across the province.

Food and Tourism Experience

Visitors can enjoy fresh seafood dishes such as grilled fish, shrimp, crabs, oysters, and local Pangasinan specialties while attending the festival.

Many food stalls and coastal restaurants participate in the event, making it a great time for a food and beach trip combination.

Why Tourists Love Pista’y Dayat

Tourists are drawn to Pista’y Dayat because it combines natural beauty, cultural heritage, and community celebration in one event.

It offers a relaxed yet meaningful experience where visitors can enjoy beaches while learning about local traditions and environmental care.

Travel Tip

Visit early in the morning for beach activities and stay until sunset to enjoy cultural shows and seaside views. Bring light clothing, sun protection, and a camera for coastal scenery.
