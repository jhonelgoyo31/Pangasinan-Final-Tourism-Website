About the Province

Gateway to the North

Pangasinan is a coastal province in Northern Luzon known for its salt-making heritage, island destinations, churches, farms, food traditions, and welcoming communities.

Gatewayto the North
LocationIlocos Region, Luzon, facing Lingayen Gulf and the West Philippine Sea.
Short backgroundThe province grew from coastal trade, salt-making, agriculture, fishing, and resilient local communities.
Known attractionsHundred Islands, Bolinao, Lingayen heritage sites, churches, eco-farms, and rice terraces.
Culture and traditionsFestivals, faith celebrations, coastal livelihoods, local cuisine, and warm Pangasinense hospitality.
Why visitPangasinan offers beaches, food, history, nature, and pilgrimage routes in one flexible itinerary.
