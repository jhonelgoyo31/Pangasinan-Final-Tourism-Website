Calasiao Puto

One of the most famous “kakanin” (Filipino rice cakes) in the Philippines, Calasiao Puto is a soft, bite-sized steamed rice cake known for its delicate sweetness and melt-in-your-mouth texture.

  • Where to try it: Calasiao, Pangasinan
  • Best known for: Soft, fluffy steamed rice cakes
  • Flavor: Lightly sweet, soft, and slightly chewy
  • Popular as: Pasalubong (souvenir food)

What is Calasiao Puto?

Calasiao Puto is a traditional Filipino steamed rice cake made from fermented rice dough. It is famous for its soft, fluffy texture and slightly sweet taste. Unlike other types of puto, Calasiao Puto is smaller, more delicate, and often eaten in one or two bites.

It is considered one of Pangasinan’s most iconic delicacies and a source of pride for the town of Calasiao.

Why Calasiao Puto is Famous

The secret behind Calasiao Puto’s popularity is the traditional fermentation process used by local makers. This process gives the rice cake its unique aroma, softness, and slightly tangy sweetness.

It has become so popular that visitors often travel to Calasiao just to buy freshly made puto from local vendors.

How It is Made

The process starts with ground rice that is naturally fermented for several hours or days. The batter is then poured into small molds and steamed until it becomes fluffy and soft.

Some modern versions add cheese, salted egg, or pandan flavor, but the original version remains the most popular.

Where to Buy Calasiao Puto

You can find Calasiao Puto in almost every bakery, roadside stall, and market in Calasiao, Pangasinan. Vendors often sell them freshly steamed in the morning and late afternoon.

Many travelers also buy it as “pasalubong” because it is affordable and easy to bring home.

Taste Experience

Calasiao Puto is soft, airy, and slightly sweet with a subtle fermented flavor. It is not overly sugary, making it perfect for pairing with savory dishes like dinuguan or grilled meats.

Why Tourists Love It

Tourists love Calasiao Puto because it is simple yet uniquely Filipino. Its soft texture and authentic preparation make it one of the most memorable food experiences in Pangasinan.

Travel Tip

Buy Calasiao Puto early in the morning for the freshest batch. It tastes best when freshly steamed and warm.
