Pigar-pigar

A beloved street food from Pangasinan, pigar-pigar is more than just a quick meal—it is a reflection of the province’s vibrant street food culture and hospitality.

  • Where to try it: Mangaldan, Dagupan City, Calasiao roadside eateries
  • Best known for: Flash-fried beef strips with onions
  • Cooking style: High-heat stir frying for crisp edges and juicy center
  • Best paired with: Rice, soup, or fried noodles

What is Pigar-pigar?

Pigar-pigar is a local Pangasinan dish made by quickly frying thin slices of beef or carabeef in very hot oil. Onions are added to enhance flavor and aroma. The cooking process is fast, often taking only a few minutes, which is why it is commonly sold in busy roadside stalls.

The dish is known for its contrast of textures—crispy edges on the meat while keeping the inside tender and juicy. It is usually cooked fresh per order, giving it a distinct hot-off-the-pan appeal.

Where It Comes From

Pigar-pigar is strongly associated with Dagupan City and nearby towns such as Mangaldan. It started as a practical street food for workers and travelers who needed a quick, filling, and affordable meal.

Over time, it became a staple in Pangasinan food culture and is now considered one of the province’s signature street dishes, alongside bangus-based meals.

How It Is Served

Traditionally, pigar-pigar is served on a plate with steamed rice and sometimes a bowl of hot soup. Some modern food stalls also serve it with pancit or garlic rice for a more filling meal.

In roadside eateries, it is often cooked in large woks right in front of customers, adding to the experience of watching the food being prepared fresh.

Taste Experience

The flavor of pigar-pigar is savory, slightly salty, and aromatic from the onions. The high-heat cooking gives the beef a lightly crispy texture while keeping it tender inside. It is simple but deeply satisfying.

Best Time to Eat

Pigar-pigar is commonly enjoyed during lunch or late afternoon. Many local stalls become busy during these hours, especially along highways and city food strips.

Why Tourists Love It

Visitors enjoy pigar-pigar because it offers an authentic taste of everyday Pangasinan life. It is affordable, freshly cooked, and widely available, making it a must-try for food travelers.

Travel Tip

For the best experience, try pigar-pigar in a busy local eatery where it is cooked fresh per order. Pair it with a cold drink and eat it immediately while it is still hot.
