Bangus (Dagupan Milkfish)

Known as the “Milkfish Capital of the Philippines,” Dagupan City is famous for producing the freshest and most flavorful bangus in the country.

  • Where to try it: Dagupan City, Pangasinan
  • Best known for: Grilled, stuffed, fried, and marinated milkfish dishes
  • Nickname: Milkfish Capital of the Philippines
  • Famous event: Bangus Festival (April)

What is Bangus?

Bangus, or milkfish, is the national fish of the Philippines and one of the most important seafood products in Pangasinan. It is known for its mild flavor, tender meat, and versatility in many Filipino dishes.

In Dagupan, bangus is raised in brackish water fishponds where the mix of saltwater and freshwater gives it a unique taste that is highly prized across the country.

Why Dagupan Bangus is Special

Dagupan bangus is considered the best in the Philippines because of its fat content and freshness. The fishpond environment in the area creates ideal growing conditions, making the meat richer and softer.

Many local families in Dagupan depend on bangus farming as their primary livelihood, making it both a cultural and economic symbol of the province.

Popular Bangus Dishes

Bangus can be prepared in many ways, each highlighting its natural flavor:

  • Inihaw na Bangus – grilled over charcoal
  • Rellenong Bangus – stuffed milkfish with vegetables and meat
  • Bangus Sisig – spicy chopped fish dish
  • Fried Bangus – crispy and golden
  • Sinigang na Bangus – sour soup with vegetables


Bangus Festival

Every April, Dagupan celebrates the Bangus Festival, one of the most colorful events in Pangasinan. It features street dancing, giant grill events, cooking competitions, and cultural performances.

The highlight is the world’s largest barbecue event where thousands of bangus are grilled along city streets.

Where to Eat Bangus

Visitors can enjoy bangus in restaurants, street food stalls, and fish markets around Dagupan City. Many eateries serve it freshly cooked from nearby fishponds.

Travel Tip

For the best experience, try eating bangus early in the morning when fish deliveries are fresh. Pair it with garlic rice and local vinegar dip for a true Pangasinan taste.
